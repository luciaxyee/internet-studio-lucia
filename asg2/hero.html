The Wild Robot: The Heart of Steel

The Robot that Learned Empathy

Overview

The Wild Robot is a part of a film that is adapted from the book entitled The Wild Robot, by Peter Brown. The movie was released in 2024, following the book's release in 2016. The visuals of this movie are very painterly and embrace the imperfect stroke marks of traditional materials and techniques that convey the softness of the natural world. The style of the Wild Robot is more rigid, but as she continues to move through the world, moss starts to grow on her as her character does. This character becomes a soft and understanding character from a rigid and closed minded character, which makes the character lovable because of the drastic change for good, for understanding emotions and others.

Journey

The Wild Robot is washed up upon the shore of a fully natural island that has no human civilization. She continues to explore and tries to find a sure fire goal that she needs to accomplish in order to return to what she thinks is where she belongs. The Wild Robot is disruptive to the natural environment and is not received well, she ends up crashing into a tree which is where she finds an egg. After moments of inner challenges of whether or not she wants to commit to this newly hatched chick, she decides to become its mother and help the chick to migrate even if it means overriding her inner truths. In the end, she ends up growing a strong sense of emotion towards those in the forest and a love towards the chick. Themes

Through her character development, themes of understanding, change, and sacrifice are presented. She learns to understand by taking time to be still and listen. She learns to change herself to better understand those around her instead of being stubborn. She learns to sacrifice parts of herself to better love the ones she learned to care about. Overall, the themes of this character challenges the inner workings of ourselves and challenges us to want to change, rather than being closed minded.
